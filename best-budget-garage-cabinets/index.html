Quick answer

The Edsal 36-inch steel cabinet is our top pick for a budget garage cabinet thanks to its rock-solid build and very affordable price.

The Craftsman 42-inch model is the editor’s pick if you want a bit more capacity and adjustable shelves, while the NewAge Pro Series is the runner-up for those willing to spend a little more for a polished look.

A garage cabinet with doors is one of the best ways to tame clutter without turning your workspace into an eyesore. Unlike open shelving, a locked cabinet keeps dust off your gear and hides the mess, all while standing up to temperature swings and occasional bumps. The trick is finding one that balances strength, capacity, and cost, especially if you’re outfitting a whole garage on a budget. When shopping on a tight budget, focus on three things: gauge of steel (thicker is better for heavy loads), overall height and depth (taller cabinets use vertical space better), and shelf adjustability. Lighter-gauge steel can sway or dent under weight, so look for models that at least use 20-gauge or thicker panels. Also consider assembly, some cabinets arrive mostly assembled while others require hours of work.
