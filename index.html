How we choose our picks

We do not run our own lab tests or timed trials. Instead, we spend weeks combing through verified owner reviews, forum discussions from gearheads and contractors, and hands-on write-ups from reputable tool sites. We look for consistent patterns: which hinges survive daily opening, which shelves stay flat under heavy drill cases, and which finishes don’t bubble or rust after two seasonal cycles in an attached garage. A cabinet earns a spot here only if long-term owners (those with a year or more of use) overwhelmingly report that it keeps its structure, that the doors align and lock properly, and that the weight rating is honest. We also factor in how easy the cabinet is to assemble and whether the manufacturer supports it with replacement parts. If a model is known for flimsy cam locks or bending middle shelves, we drop it, no matter how glossy the marketing photos look.
